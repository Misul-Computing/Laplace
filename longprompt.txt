The history of computing spans several decades of innovation. In the 1940s, ENIAC was built at the University of Pennsylvania using vacuum tubes. Alan Turing published his seminal paper on computable numbers in 1936, laying the theoretical foundation. The transistor was invented at Bell Labs in 1947 by Bardeen, Brattain, and Shockley. Integrated circuits followed in 1958, developed independently by Kilby and Noyce. The 1970s saw the microprocessor revolution with Intel's 4004. Personal computers emerged in the 1980s with the IBM PC and Apple's Macintosh. The internet grew from ARPANET in 1969 to global adoption by the late 1990s. Tim Berners-Lee created the World Wide Web at CERN in 1989. The 2000s brought smartphones, starting with the iPhone in 2007. Cloud computing transformed infrastructure in the 2010s. Machine learning advances accelerated with deep neural networks, culminating in transformer architectures after 2017. Large language models like GPT-3 demonstrated emergent capabilities at scale. Today, artificial intelligence is reshaping software development, scientific research, and creative industries.
